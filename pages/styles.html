Solving Styles

Blindfolded


Perhaps one of the most impressive ways of solving is with a blindfold on. The person solving the cube is timed while they inspect the cube. They can only make turns after donning a blindfold. Their completion time is the inspection time plus the solve time.

One-handed


This style is exactly what it sounds like: everything is the same, just use one less hand. Surprisingly, this actually changes the common algorithms that top notch cubers use. Some algorithms that work well with two hands simply aren't viable with one hand.

Fewest Moves


If speedcubing doesn't impress, then perhaps this type of solving will. Competitors get one hour to come up with a solution to a mixed up cube, and the solution with the fewest moves (or steps) wins. Much slower paced with far more analytics.
